Title:To Kill a Mockingbird

Author: Harper Lee

Set in the 1930s, this Pulitzer Prize-winning novel follows Scout Finch, a young girl growing up in the racially segregated town of Maycomb, Alabama. Her father, Atticus Finch, is a lawyer who defends a Black man falsely accused of raping a white woman. Through Scout's eyes, the book explores themes of racism, morality, and justice. To Kill a Mockingbird remains a powerful commentary on prejudice and human empathy.
